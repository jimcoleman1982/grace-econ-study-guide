**Module Objectives: International Trade**

**Upon completion of this module, you will be able to:**

1\. Explain how nations gain from specialization and trade.

2\. Given the Supply and Demand model, illustrate an export market vs an import market.

3\. Discuss the benefits and costs of international trade.

4\. Evaluate protectionism and barriers to trade.

5\. Analyze the effects of tariffs and quotas with respect to benefits and costs.

6\. Given the Supply and Demand model, illustrate a tariff graphically.

**Module Objectives: International Finance** 

**Upon completion of this module, you will be able to:**

1\. Explain a country’s trade balance and identify patterns in US trade deficits and surpluses.

2\. Explain the balance of payment system, including the current account and capital (financial) account, and the connection between trade balances and international financial capital flows.

3\. Using supply and demand, explain how the exchange rate is determined and why it fluctuates.

4\. Differentiate between exchange rate policies, including a floating exchange rate, a soft peg, a hard peg, and a merged currency.

**Module Objectives: Economic growth and productivity** 

**Upon completion of this module, you will be able to:**

1\. Define economic growth, productivity, and the standard of living.

2\. Explain the fundamental determinants of economic growth and changes in productivity as it relates to the standard of living.

3\. Use “the Rule of 72” to evaluate growth rates.

4\. Discuss the preconditions for growth & keys to economic development.

5\. Evaluate public policy options to achieve faster economic growth.

**Module Objectives: Aggregate supply and aggregate demand**

**Upon completion of this module, you will be able to:**

1\. Illustrate the aggregate supply/aggregate demand model and influences & shifts on the model.

2\. Use the AS/AD model to analyze recessionary and inflationary gaps.

3\. Using the model, compare demand-pull v. cost-push inflation.

4\. Evaluate the dilemma government faces when trying to combat stagflation.

5\. Compare and contrast Keynesian and Neoclassical perspectives.
